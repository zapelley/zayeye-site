Assess, then build.

Every engagement follows the same logic. We never start with a tool; we start with how your work actually flows. And everything we build follows one architecture rule — the durable value (your project data, your standards, your firm's judgment, encoded) belongs to you. The AI component is deliberately replaceable. Own the asset; rent nothing that matters.

Tier 1 — the front door

AI Opportunity Assessment

What it is
A fixed-scope diagnostic. Structured interviews with your office and field leads, maps of how work actually flows (not how the org chart says it flows), a scored and prioritized matrix of where AI genuinely pays, a phased roadmap — and one recommended first build.
Who it's for
A small to mid-size GC or delivery firm that suspects it's leaking hours but wants evidence before spending on tools.
What changes
You get your own numbers — where the hours go, what they cost, and what's worth fixing first. The report stands alone: act on it with us, with someone else, or not at all.

Tier 2 — prove it

Pilot build

What it is
One workflow, one working tool, delivered into your operation — typically the assessment's recommended first build. Deliberately engineered as a slice of a larger foundation: if you continue, nothing is thrown away.
Who it's for
Firms ready to prove value on one painful workflow — submittal handling, report generation, document retrieval, a pursuit library — before committing wider.
What changes
A task that took hours takes minutes, in a tool your team runs and your firm owns. Real usage data replaces speculation about what AI can do for you.

Tier 3 — make it how you run

Implementation & advisory

What it is
The full build-out on the roadmap — the project's information substrate plus the generators on top of it (registers, reports, drafts) — with ongoing advisory and training as your team takes it over.
Who it's for
Firms past the pilot, ready to make this how projects run.
What changes
Your project information stops being scattered across sources of truth. Documents that were assembled by hand get generated from one substrate — and the system compounds as more projects flow through it.

How every build is engineered

Most of what we deliver isn't AI at all. The bulk of any system is ordinary engineering — structured files and traditional code that put your project's facts in one place. A smaller layer encodes your firm's standards and judgment as explicit rules. The AI is the smallest piece by design: it sits in one clearly bounded slot a person could fill, so every output stays reviewable and the model itself stays swappable. That's why these systems are reliable, auditable, and yours — correctness comes from the foundation, not from the model.

The first step is a conversation, not a contract.
